LCG Recurrence
$Z_{n+1} = (aZ_n + c) \bmod m$
Runs Test
$\mu_r = \frac{2ab}{n} + 0.5$,   $\sigma_r^2 = \frac{2ab(2ab-n)}{n^2(n-1)}$
Chi-Squared Statistic
$\chi^2 = \sum \frac{(N_j - np_j)^2}{np_j}$
Poisson PMF
$P[N(t)=k] = \frac{(\lambda t)^k}{k!} e^{-\lambda t}$
Exponential Distribution
$f(t) = \lambda e^{-\lambda t}$,   mean $= 1/\lambda$
t-Test Statistic
$t = \frac{\bar{X}(n) - \mu_0}{SE}$
Confidence Interval
$\bar{X}(n) \pm t_{n-1,1-\alpha/2} \cdot SE$
Welch Degrees of Freedom
$\hat{f} = \frac{(S_1^2/n_1 + S_2^2/n_2)^2}{\frac{(S_1^2/n_1)^2}{n_1-1} + \frac{(S_2^2/n_2)^2}{n_2-1}}$
Hitchhiker Estimator
$\frac{1}{2}\!\left(\beta + \frac{\sigma^2}{\beta}\right)$

💡 Study Tips

  • 1 Start with the foundations. Topics 1 and 2 (RNGs and testing) underpin everything else. Make sure you can manually compute an LCG sequence and perform a runs test before moving on.
  • 2 Practice manual calculations. The exam is closed book with only a calculator. Work through every formula by hand at least once -- especially chi-squared tests, confidence intervals, and Welch's t-test.
  • 3 Understand when to use each test. Know the decision criteria: paired vs. unpaired, normal vs. non-parametric, one-sided vs. two-sided. The True/False section often tests these distinctions.
  • 4 Manage your time carefully. The 2-hour exam has 8 questions worth 98 points -- roughly 15 minutes per question. If you get stuck, move on and come back. Partial credit is awarded for correct intermediate steps.
  • 5 Use the quizzes on each page. Each topic has two quizzes (4 questions each). Aim for perfect scores -- they test exactly the types of questions that appear on the exam.
  • 6 Use your formula sheet. You're allowed a formula sheet during the exam. Look for the amber 📋 FS: badges throughout the topic pages — they tell you exactly which formula sheet section to reference for each concept.

✅ Partial Credit Tips

  • Always show intermediate steps — partial marks for correct working
  • Label which formula/test you are using
  • If stuck on a sub-part, assume a reasonable answer and continue
  • Check: do probabilities sum to 1? Do counts match expected totals?
  • For CIs: state the confidence level, d.f., and interpret in context
  • For True/False: the justification is worth more than the answer

⚠ Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Forgetting to take mod in LCG calculations
  • Wrong degrees of freedom in chi-squared ($k-1$, not $k$)
  • Confusing $\lambda$ (rate) with $1/\lambda$ (mean) in exponentials
  • Inverting the wrong CDF piece for piecewise distributions
  • Using z-critical instead of t-critical when $\sigma$ is unknown
  • Forgetting to subtract estimated parameters from d.f. in GoF tests
  • Not pairing observations when CRN was used
  • Bootstrap: using $j = Np$ instead of $j = Np + 0.5$
  • Claiming "fail to reject" means "$H_0$ is true"
